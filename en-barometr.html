How to read fluctuations in practice?

Truck availability (Where are you loading?)

Example: You are shipping goods from Belgium.

Is Belgium glowing green?

Deviation favoring loads. Truck availability is lower than usual for this region. Carriers can cherry-pick offers, making it harder and more time-consuming to find an available truck.

Is Belgium glowing red?

Deviation favoring trucks. There is higher truck availability than usual. In this market, you will find an available carrier for your load much faster and easier.

Transport cost (Where are you delivering?)

Example: The destination dictates pricing pressure.

Shipping to a red zone?

Rates go up. Carriers know it is harder to find a return load in the destination region (unusual excess of trucks), so they expect compensation through a higher freight rate.

Shipping to a green zone?

Downward pressure on rates. This is a highly desirable destination – carriers know there are more offers waiting there than usual, so they are willing to go cheaper for a guaranteed return trip.

Lacking available trucks or rates are rising?

Even with strong market fluctuations and a lack of vehicles in your region, I can secure the required fleet. I will organize a dedicated transport based on a solid base of established carrier contacts.
